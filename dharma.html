Dharma Project

There is nothing more fulfilling than sharing the technology of kundalini yoga with youth.

When I consider the role that yoga has played in helping me grow into a happier, healthier adult, I can’t help but wish that I had access to yoga and meditation in my youth. Fortunately, I have the opportunity to share this practice with youth. I recently worked with a group eighth grade girls at Lincoln Middle School in an 8‑week session of yoga, reading, journaling and dialoguing about what it means to be a girl in this increasingly complex society.

The young women will:

  • learn techniques for self-reflection and awareness about how they think about themselves, their patterns of behavior and reactions, and the ways they learn.
  • express their own values, interests, and feelings in written form (journals) and through other forms of creative expression.
  • learn productive ways of interacting with those around them (adults and peers).
  • practice breathing exercises, chanting, and yoga kriyas (sequenced physical poses) as a form of strengthening muscles, increasing flexibility, and calming the mind and body.
  • analyze and critique social, print, and video media as a means of challenging negative stereotypes and defining their own space in the world around them.
If you would like to bring a youth workshop to your school, please contact me: kundalini@kimberlynao.com.
